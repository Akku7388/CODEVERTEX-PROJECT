Early Life

Dr. Bheemrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was born on 14 April 1891 in Mhow, India. He was a social reformer, jurist, economist, politician, and the principal architect of the Indian Constitution. Born into a Dalit family, he faced severe discrimination but went on to become one of India's most influential leaders.

Contributions

  • Father of the Indian Constitution: Ambedkar was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Indian Constitution. His efforts were instrumental in shaping modern India's legal and political structure.
  • Social Reforms: He tirelessly worked for the upliftment of the Dalits and other marginalized communities, advocating for their rights and fighting against social discrimination.
  • Economic Thought: Ambedkar's economic ideas and thoughts on labor, land, and agricultural reforms were ahead of his time and contributed significantly to the economic policies of independent India.

Legacy

Dr. Ambedkar passed away on 6 December 1956, leaving behind a legacy of justice, equality, and social reform. His life's work continues to inspire millions across the globe. In 1990, he was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
